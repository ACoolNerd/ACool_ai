#### ✅ ACoolBUSINESS IRS Entity Classification (LLC vs. S-Corp)
- [ ] **Status:** Not started
- **Primary Purpose:** Choose optimal tax treatment
- **Decision Tree:**

```
ACoolBUSINESS Entity Classification
│
├─→ DISREGARDED ENTITY (Default for single-member LLC)
│   ✓ No separate tax return; report on owner's personal return (Schedule C)
│   ✓ Simple administration
│   ✗ Doesn't separate business and personal income
│   ✗ All net profit subject to self-employment tax (~15.3%)
│   Best for: Low-income, no employees
│
├─→ LLC TAXED AS C-CORPORATION
│   ✓ Separate tax entity (Form 1120)
│   ✓ Liability protection + business separation
│   ✗ Double taxation: Corp tax (21%) + dividend tax (20% + 3.8% NIIT)
│   ✓ Can retain earnings (defer distribution to reduce personal tax)
│   Best for: High-income, reinvesting profits
│
└─→ LLC TAXED AS S-CORPORATION ⭐ RECOMMENDED
    ✓ Pass-through taxation (no corporate-level tax)
    ✓ Self-employment tax savings (only W-2 salary is subject to 15.3% tax)
    ✓ Dividends/distributions NOT subject to self-employment tax
    ✓ Separates business and personal liability
    ✗ Requires reasonable W-2 salary (IRS scrutiny)
    ✗ Payroll processing (quarterly filings, W-2/1099)
    Example: $100K profit
      - Disregarded/C-Corp: Self-employment tax ~$14.1K
      - S-Corp: W-2 salary $60K (tax ~$9.2K) + $40K distribution (no tax) = Savings ~$5K
```

**ACoolBUSINESS Recommendation: S-Corporation Election**

- [ ] **Action:** File Form 2553 (S-Corp election) within 2.5 months of forming LLC OR by March 15 following tax year
- [ ] **Form:** https://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-pdf/f2553.pdf
- [ ] **State:** Maryland also requires S-Corp election (form CT-1 or similar)

#### ✅ Paying Employees, Shareholders & Corporate Officers (S-Corp Tax Treatment)
- [ ] **Status:** Not started
- **Primary Purpose:** Understand payroll tax obligations and reasonable compensation
- **Key Concept: Reasonable Salary**
  - If electing S-Corp tax treatment, corporate officers MUST take a "reasonable W-2 salary"
  - IRS defines reasonable as: comparable to what similar businesses pay for similar roles
  - Typical threshold: 50% of net profit (varies by industry)
  - Example: If net profit is $100K, reasonable W-2 salary is $50K–$70K
  - Excess profit can be distributed as dividends (not subject to self-employment tax)
  - **Penalty for underpaying:** IRS reclassifies dividends as hidden wages + penalties + interest

- **Payroll Taxes (if taking W-2 salary):**
  - Employee portion: 7.65% (6.2% Social Security + 1.45% Medicare) — withheld from paycheck
  - Employer portion: 7.65% — paid by business
  - Total: 15.3% of W-2 wages
  - Quarterly filing: Form 941 (federal), state equivalent
  - Annual filing: Form 940 (federal unemployment), W-2 (employee copy), W-3 (summary)

- **Distribution/Dividend Taxes (if electing S-Corp):**
  - No self-employment tax on distributions
  - Reported on K-1 (Pass-Through Entity Reporting)
  - Owner reports on personal return (Form 1040, Schedule E)
  - Subject to income tax (not self-employment tax)

- **Payroll Service Options:**
  - ADP, Gusto, Paychex, OnPay
  - Cost: $20–$50/month per employee
  - Handles: W-2 withholding, quarterly filing, annual reporting
