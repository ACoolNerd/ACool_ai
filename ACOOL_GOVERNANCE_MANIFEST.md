#### 20. ACoolBUSINESS IRS Entity Classification (LLC vs. S-Corp)
- **Purpose:** Elect optimal tax treatment (LLC taxed as C-Corp, S-Corp, or disregarded entity)
- **Status:** [ ] Document needed
- **Decision Tree:**
  - **Disregarded Entity (default for single-member LLC):** Owner reports all income/loss on personal return. No separate tax return. Simple but no separation of business and personal income.
  - **C-Corporation Election:** Separate tax return (Form 1120). Double taxation risk but liability protection. Better for retained earnings strategy.
  - **S-Corporation Election (Form 2553):** Pass-through taxation like partnership but with self-employment tax savings (if done correctly). Requires active participation and reasonable W-2 salary.
  - **Recommendation for ACoolBUSINESS:** [ ] Determine optimal based on income projection and distribution plans.

**Link to Form 2553 (S-Corp Election):** https://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-pdf/f2553.pdf

### Payroll, Distributions & Officer Compensation

#### 22. Paying Employees, Shareholders & Corporate Officers (S-Corp Tax Treatment)
- **Purpose:** Understand payroll tax obligations and reasonable compensation requirements
- **Status:** [ ] Document needed
- **Key Concepts:**
  - **W-2 Wages (Salary):** Corporate officers must take reasonable W-2 salary subject to payroll taxes (employee + employer).
  - **1099 Distributions:** Remaining profit (after W-2 salary) can be distributed as dividends or equity distributions with no self-employment tax.
  - **Reasonable Salary Requirement:** IRS requires corporate officers to pay themselves a "reasonable salary" — not just distributions. Typical threshold: 50%+ of net profit.
  - **Penalty for Underpaying:** IRS can reclassify distributions as hidden W-2 wages with penalties and back taxes.

**Example (S-Corp with $100K net profit):**
  - Reasonable W-2 salary: $60K (payroll taxes ~$9.18K total)
  - Remaining distribution: $40K (no self-employment tax)
  - vs. Sole proprietor: $100K (self-employment tax ~$14.1K)
  - **Savings:** ~$5K per year (tax planning opportunity)

**Link to IRS S-Corp Reasonable Compensation Guide:** https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/s-corporations

**Link to Form W-2 (Wage & Tax Statement):** https://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-pdf/fw2.pdf

**Link to Form 1099-DIV (Dividend Distribution):** https://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-pdf/f1099div.pdf

## Rights → Disclosure → Proof: Governance Principle

Every decision in the ACoolECOSYSTEM follows this exact order:

### 1. RIGHTS (Before any decision)
- Who has the authority to decide?
- What does the operating agreement/trust document say?
- Are all required approvals in place?

### 2. DISCLOSURE (When the decision is made)
- Notify all stakeholders
- Document the decision in writing
- File required forms (IRS, state, beneficiaries)
- Archive in permanent record

### 3. PROOF (After implementation)
- Tax return filed and accepted
- Distributions paid and receipted
- Beneficiary acknowledgment
- Audit trail for review

**Example: ACoolBUSINESS to ACoolHOLDINGS Asset Transfer**

| Phase | Action | Document |
|-------|--------|----------|
| **Rights** | Confirm ACoolTRUST is member of ACoolHOLDINGS | Operating agreement |
| | Confirm manager (Keith) has authority to transfer assets | Manager authority resolution |
| **Disclosure** | Notify beneficiaries of transfer | Memo to ACoolTRUST beneficiaries |
| | File transfer documentation | State-filed amendment (if required) |
| | Report to IRS on tax return | Form 8594 or Schedule C |
| **Proof** | Tax return filed and accepted | IRS acceptance notice |
| | Distribution to beneficiaries documented | Distribution memo + receipt |
| | Annual audit by CPA | Audit opinion (if applicable) |
